Career and Educational Path

A Quick Look Into My Professional and Academic Career

  • Full Stack Engineer 03/20 - Present

    UCLA Extension

    One of my best friends started a Full Stack Engineering BootCamp in Seattle which caught my eye. I spoke to him in depth about it, and decided to do some research myself. As I continued to look into Software Development, the more I started to get hooked! I started to do free courses online which I got better and better over time, and in doing so I thought about really investing in myself to learn the industry. With steep prices, I hesitated a lot, but the more I thought about it I knew this was the industry I wanted to be in and make a long-time career out of it. I started attending the UCLA Extension Full Stack Engineering Course which unfortunately started at the beginning of the COVID-19 Pandemic. But, sitting where I am at now, this may have been a blessing in disguise. Learning such a difficult skill online helped me to become more discipline, self-reliant, and persistent. I was able to work with real clients that we vetted ourselves and deliver products that I will forever be proud of. I graduate this September 2020, and I cannot wait to apply what I have learned in a company where I can contribute at a high level

  • First Job In LA!

    LoopUp

    With JOEY Restaurants, I had the opportunity to help open the LA branch up. Going through my years living in LA, I decided to leave the Restaurant Industry and get into Tech World. For all those who have decided to pivot in their career, you definitely understand how hard it was for me to land a position at a tech company. Luckily, right before I left for the holidays I received my first offer from a company called LoopUp. In my position at LoopUp, I assisted in Sales Meetings, learned how to talk to C-Class Offiers and IT Directors, and, most importantly, learned how to fail....A LOT! Sales is no joke of a job and definitely teaches you how to be persistent and resilient in life.

  • 2014-2018 || Management

    JOEY Restaurant Group

    This is my first management position that I held in my professional career. I was managing 100+ partners and was responsible for their development in their own career steps. The most valuable thing that I will take away from JOEY, is how to be coachable. I grew up competitive. I played sports my whole life, won state twice and got a scholarship to continue my athletic career. Being competitive and being Number One was engrained in my DNA. So when I came across a company that pointed out the flaws and things that I needed to work on, I immediately became defensive. This was something that I worked on during my tenure at JOEY, and proudly still work on today. I believe it is important for everyone to accept and own the things that dont make them great, whatever they may be

Soma Kitchen LA

Deployed Website for Client

Soma Kitchen is a catering company located in Los Angeles. They had a website with no functionality. The owners wanted a website that allowed customers to engage with their products and services without having to reach out to them via phone or social media. We implemented several web APIs that allowed clients to order from their online menu, book private events, and pay for whatever products or services they have chose. We also utilized a MySQL database to keep track of previous, current, and inquiring customers.

  • Live Link: Soma Kitchen
  • Technologies Used: Handlebars.js, CSS, Node.js, Express, MySQL, Sequelize ORM, Square API, Google Calendar API

Law Offices of Theodore Behlendorf

Deployed Website for Client

With no existing website, we were tasked to create a website that would increase their online presence. We wanted to work with a few web APIs that would give their website a little more interaction and say a little bit more of what type of Law they specialize in. We utilized a Twitter API to have a live timeline about Immigration Law. In the footer, we used an OpenLayers API to show the locaiton of their law firm.

  • Live Link: Law Office
  • Technologies Used: HTML, CSS, Javascript, Materialize, jQuery, Twitter API, OpenLayers API, SmptJS

Weather Dashboard

Application

Created a weather dashboard that would takes in a user input of any city in the world. After the input is received, the dashboard will display the current weather and gives you a 5 day forecast. The application utilizes JSON format to give you all the information that you need. As the user inputs more cities, all the previous searches will be stored and displayed on the left hand side of the application.

  • Live Link: Weather Dashboard
  • Technologies Used: HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, Javascript, jQuery, AJAX, OpenWeather API, Moment.js
